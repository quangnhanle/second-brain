## 🎯 Band 7.0 Model Essay

It is sometimes argued that environmental issues are too vast for any single person to address, whereas others insist that individuals can genuinely make a difference. While both arguments have merit, I believe that individual efforts and [[Ielts/Vocabulary/Topic/Environment#🌍 Environment — Vocabulary|collective action]] are equally important.

On the one hand, it is understandable why many people feel powerless in the face of [[Ielts/Vocabulary/Topic/Environment#🌍 Environment — Vocabulary|environmental degradation]]. Problems such as climate change, deforestation and ocean pollution operate [[Ielts/Vocabulary/Topic/Environment#🌍 Environment — Vocabulary|on a global scale]] and are largely driven by industrial activity and government policy. A single person, for instance, cannot prevent factories from emitting [[Ielts/Vocabulary/Topic/Environment#🌍 Environment — Vocabulary|greenhouse gases]] or stop large corporations from clearing rainforests for profit. From this perspective, meaningful solutions appear to depend on [[Ielts/Vocabulary/Topic/Environment#🌍 Environment — Vocabulary|international agreements]], such as the Paris Agreement, rather than on the actions of ordinary citizens.

On the other hand, I would argue that individuals play a crucial role precisely because change often begins at the [[Ielts/Vocabulary/Topic/Environment#🌍 Environment — Vocabulary|grassroots level]]. When people adopt [[Ielts/Vocabulary/Topic/Environment#🌍 Environment — Vocabulary|environmentally friendly]] habits — reducing meat consumption, using public transport or recycling — they not only lower their own impact but also influence those around them. Moreover, the combined choices of millions of consumers can pressure companies to adopt greener practices, since businesses ultimately respond to demand. In this way, [[Ielts/Vocabulary/Topic/Environment#🌍 Environment — Vocabulary|personal responsibility]] gradually translates into wider social and political change.

In conclusion, although environmental problems are undoubtedly too large for any individual to solve alone, this does not render personal action meaningless. On the contrary, I believe that genuine progress can only be achieved when responsible individuals and decisive government policies work together.
