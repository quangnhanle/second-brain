## 🏆 B. Model Answer — Band 7.0+

*(Some people think that the best way to solve global environmental problems is to increase the cost of fuel. To what extent do you agree or disagree?)*

It is sometimes argued that the most effective way to tackle global environmental problems is to raise the price of fuel. While I agree that higher fuel costs can reduce pollution to some extent, I do not believe this is the best solution on its own; rather, it should form part of a wider set of measures.

On the one hand, increasing fuel prices can clearly discourage harmful consumption. When petrol and diesel become more expensive, running a private car is no longer affordable for many people, so they are likely to turn to cleaner alternatives such as public transport, electric vehicles or car-sharing schemes. This shift reduces the volume of carbon dioxide released into the atmosphere, which in turn improves air quality and public health. Japan, for instance, combines relatively high fuel taxes with an extensive railway network, and as a result a large proportion of commuters choose trains over private cars, helping to limit traffic-related emissions.

On the other hand, this approach has significant drawbacks and cannot solve the problem alone. Firstly, it tends to be socially unfair: because fuel is essential for transporting goods and powering industry, higher fuel prices push up the cost of everyday products. This burden falls most heavily on low-income households, whereas wealthier people can easily absorb the extra expense. Secondly, raising fuel prices fails to address the root causes of environmental damage, much of which stems from industrial production, power generation and deforestation rather than private travel. In my view, governments should therefore prioritise investment in renewable energy, expand public transport infrastructure and impose stricter regulations on industrial emissions.

In conclusion, although increasing the cost of fuel can reduce pollution to a certain degree, it is neither a fair nor a complete solution. To protect the environment effectively, this policy should be combined with renewable energy and broader, well-targeted measures.
