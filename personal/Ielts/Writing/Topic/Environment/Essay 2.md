**Some people think that the best way to solve global environmental problems is to increase the cost of fuel. To what extent do you agree or disagree?**

Dàn ý:
> Dạng đề: **Opinion (agree/disagree)** — Position: **Partly agree** (tăng giá nhiên liệu có ích nhưng không phải cách tốt nhất, cần kết hợp giải pháp khác).

	- **Para 1 — Introduction**
		- Paraphrase the question: `A number of people believe that raising the price of fuel is the most effective solution to the world's environmental challenges.`
		- State your position clearly: `While higher fuel costs can reduce pollution to some extent, I would argue that this measure alone is not the best solution and should be combined with other approaches.`
	- **Para 2 — Body 1: Why higher fuel prices DO help (mặt đồng ý)**
		- Topic sentence: Higher fuel prices can encourage people to pollute less.
		- Logic chain: fuel price increases -> private travel becomes expensive -> people switch to public transport / electric vehicles / car-sharing -> CO2 emissions fall
		- Result: cleaner air, better public health
		- Example: countries with high fuel taxes + strong rail systems (e.g. Japan), where most people prefer public transport
	- **Para 3 — Body 2: Why it is NOT the best solution (mặt hạn chế)**
		- Topic sentence: However, this approach has serious drawbacks and is not a complete solution.
		- Drawback 1 — social unfairness: fuel is a necessity -> higher transport costs raise the price of goods -> the burden falls hardest on low-income people, while the rich can still afford it
		- Drawback 2 — does not address the root cause: pollution also comes from industry, power generation and waste, which fuel prices do not touch
		- Better / complementary solutions: invest in renewable energy, regulate industrial emissions, subsidise EVs and public transport
	- **Para 4 — Conclusion**
		- Restate position: raising fuel costs reduces pollution to a degree but is not the best solution
		- Summarise the two main points: it changes behaviour <-> but is unfair and overlooks other pollution sources
		- Final thought: it should be combined with cleaner energy and broader policy. No new ideas.
