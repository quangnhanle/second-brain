# 💰 Money — Vocabulary

| Từ gốc | Loại từ | Phát âm | Nghĩa tiếng Việt | Nghĩa tiếng Anh | Ví dụ | Hình ảnh |
| ------ | ------- | ------- | ---------------- | --------------- | ----- | -------- |
| current account | noun phrase | /ˈkʌrənt əˈkaʊnt/ | tài khoản vãng lai | an account you use for most everyday business | I pay my bills from my **current account**. | |
| interest rate | noun phrase | /ˈɪntrəst reɪt/ | lãi suất | the percentage which the bank pays you based on how much you have | The bank offers the best **interest rates** in town. | |
| overdraft | noun | /ˈəʊvədrɑːft/ | thấu chi | permission to have a negative amount of money in your account | The bank gave me a guaranteed **overdraft** facility. | |
| mortgage | noun | /ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ/ | khoản vay thế chấp (mua nhà) | a loan used usually to buy a house | We took out a **mortgage** with low deposits. | |
| deposit | noun | /dɪˈpɒzɪt/ | tiền đặt cọc | money you pay before buying something to show you really want to buy it | They offer mortgages with low **deposits**. | |
| repayment | noun | /rɪˈpeɪmənt/ | khoản trả góp | a payment to reduce the amount you owe | The loan has flexible **repayments**. | |
| debt | noun | /det/ | nợ | money you owe someone | He consolidated all his cards and **debts** into one payment. | |
| credit limit | noun phrase | /ˈkredɪt ˈlɪmɪt/ | hạn mức tín dụng | the maximum amount of money you may spend on your card | Discuss your **credit limit** with us today. | |
| savings account | noun phrase | /ˈseɪvɪŋz əˈkaʊnt/ | tài khoản tiết kiệm | an account where you put money you do not immediately need | I've just opened a **savings account**. | |
| steady income | noun phrase | /ˈstedi ˈɪnkʌm/ | thu nhập ổn định | money coming in regularly, in a way that does not change much | I have a **steady income** now, which is great. | |
| transfer | verb | /trænsˈfɜː/ | chuyển khoản | to move money from one account to another | I **transfer** money into it regularly. | |
| outgoings | noun | /ˈaʊtɡəʊɪŋz/ | các khoản chi tiêu | money you pay each month, e.g. rent, cost of running a car | My **outgoings** are quite low. | |
| expenses | noun | /ɪkˈspensɪz/ | chi phí | money you spend on things | I don't have any major **expenses**. | |
| direct debit | noun phrase | /daɪˈrekt ˈdebɪt/ | ghi nợ tự động (trừ tiền tự động) | money taken automatically from your account, e.g. to pay bills | I pay all my bills by **direct debit**. | |
| credit rating | noun phrase | /ˈkredɪt ˈreɪtɪŋ/ | xếp hạng tín dụng | a judgement made by a bank about how likely someone is to pay back money they borrow | I have a good **credit rating**, so I can borrow money if I need to. | |
| in credit | adjective phrase | /ɪn ˈkredɪt/ | có số dư dương | having a positive amount of money in an account | My account is usually **in credit** at the beginning of the month. | |
| overdrawn | adjective | /ˌəʊvəˈdrɔːn/ | bị thấu chi (âm tài khoản) | having a negative amount of money in an account | By the end of the month it's always **overdrawn**. | |
| in the red | idiom | /ɪn ðə red/ | nợ ngân hàng (số dư âm) | having a negative amount of money in your account | I hate being **in the red**. | |
| charge interest | verb phrase | /tʃɑːdʒ ˈɪntrəst/ | tính lãi | to make you pay a percentage of the amount | The banks all **charge interest** on overdrafts. | |
| overdraft limit | noun phrase | /ˈəʊvədrɑːft ˈlɪmɪt/ | hạn mức thấu chi | the maximum negative amount you may have in your account | I try not to go over my **overdraft limit**. | |
| finance | verb | /ˈfaɪnæns/ | tài trợ, chu cấp | to find the money needed to pay for something | I'm not sure how I'm going to **finance** the car. | |
| online banking | noun phrase | /ˈɒnlaɪn ˈbæŋkɪŋ/ | ngân hàng trực tuyến | banking done over the internet | I use **online banking** most of the time. | |
| cash machine | noun phrase | /kæʃ məˈʃiːn/ | máy rút tiền (ATM) | a machine from which you can take out money | I only go to the bank occasionally, to use the **cash machine**. | |
| income tax | noun phrase | /ˈɪnkʌm tæks/ | thuế thu nhập | the tax collected on wages and salaries | **Income tax** is collected on wages and salaries. | |
| inheritance tax | noun phrase | /ɪnˈherɪtəns tæks/ | thuế thừa kế | tax collected on money people get from people who have died | **Inheritance tax** is collected on money from people who have died. | |
| customs duty | noun phrase | /ˈkʌstəmz ˈdjuːti/ | thuế hải quan | a tax paid on goods imported from other countries | **Customs duty** is paid on goods imported from other countries. | |
| duty-free | adjective | /ˌdjuːti ˈfriː/ | miễn thuế | (of goods) on which you do not have to pay tax | Airports usually have **duty-free** shops. | |
| VAT | noun | /ˌviː eɪ ˈtiː/ | thuế giá trị gia tăng | value added tax; a tax paid on most goods and services | **VAT** is a tax paid on most goods and services. | |
| corporation tax | noun phrase | /ˌkɔːpəˈreɪʃən tæks/ | thuế thu nhập doanh nghiệp | a tax companies pay on their profits | Companies pay **corporation tax** on their profits. | |
| excise duty | noun phrase | /ˈɛksaɪz ˈduti/ | thuế tiêu thụ đặc biệt | a tax charged on particular goods such as alcohol or fuel | The unit presents **excise duty** as key vocabulary. |  |
