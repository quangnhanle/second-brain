# 📈 Business — Vocabulary

| Từ gốc | Loại từ | Phát âm | Nghĩa tiếng Việt | Nghĩa tiếng Anh | Ví dụ | Hình ảnh |
| ------ | ------- | ------- | ---------------- | --------------- | ----- | -------- |
| put forward | phrasal verb | /pʊt ˈfɔːwəd/ | đề xuất, đưa ra | to offer an idea for people to consider | He **put forward** a business plan to sell custom-built computers. | |
| business plan | noun phrase | /ˈbɪznəs plæn/ | kế hoạch kinh doanh | a plan of how the business will operate | He put forward a **business plan** to his bank manager. | |
| custom-built | adjective | /ˌkʌstəm ˈbɪlt/ | làm theo yêu cầu | specially made for a particular person or thing | He sold **custom-built** computers on the Internet. | |
| launch | verb | /lɔːntʃ/ | ra mắt, khởi động | to start, often with a special event | He **launched** his business a year later. | |
| roll out | phrasal verb | /rəʊl aʊt/ | tung ra, triển khai | to make a new product available for the first time | Two years later he **rolled out** an expanded range. | |
| expand | verb | /ɪkˈspænd/ | mở rộng | to make or grow bigger | He rolled out an **expanded** range, including tablets. | |
| firm | noun | /fɜːm/ | công ty, hãng | a company | His **firm** now manufactures around 200 machines a year. | |
| manufacture | verb | /ˌmænjʊˈfæktʃə/ | sản xuất | to produce goods in large numbers, usually in a factory | His firm now **manufactures** around 200 machines a year. | |
| customer care | noun phrase | /ˈkʌstəmə keə/ | chăm sóc khách hàng | looking after the customer's needs | It's all about personal service and **customer care**. | |
| priority | noun | /praɪˈɒrəti/ | ưu tiên | something very important that must be dealt with first | This was our **priority** from the start. | |
| market research | noun phrase | /ˈmɑːkɪt rɪˈsɜːtʃ/ | nghiên cứu thị trường | finding out beforehand what people want or need | We didn't really do any proper **market research**. | |
| recession | noun | /rɪˈseʃn/ | suy thoái kinh tế | a period of low economic activity | We weren't sure if such machines would sell during a **recession**. | |
| potential | adjective | /pəˈtenʃl/ | tiềm năng | possible when the conditions are right | I felt there were enough **potential** customers. | |
| risk | noun | /rɪsk/ | rủi ro | the possibility of something bad happening | I felt it was worth the **risk**. | |
| order books | noun phrase | /ˈɔːdə bʊks/ | sổ đơn hàng | the list of orders waiting to be completed | Our **order books** were full. | |
| going forward | phrase | /ˈɡəʊɪŋ ˈfɔːwəd/ | trong tương lai, sắp tới | going into the future | **Going forward**, Archer plans to expand the business further. | |
| CEO | noun | /ˌsiː iː ˈəʊ/ | giám đốc điều hành | Chief Executive Officer | The **CEO** of a big corporation gave us these tips for success. | |
| corporation | noun | /ˌkɔːpəˈreɪʃn/ | tập đoàn | a large company or group of companies controlled together | The CEO of a big **corporation** gave us these tips. | |
| do business | verb phrase | /duː ˈbɪznəs/ | làm ăn, giao thương | to carry out commercial activity | **Do business** in the markets you know best. | |
| access new markets | verb phrase | /ˈækses njuː ˈmɑːkɪts/ | thâm nhập thị trường mới | to reach and do business with new groups of customers | It may be difficult to **access new markets**. | |
| build contacts | verb phrase | /bɪld ˈkɒntækts/ | xây dựng quan hệ | to develop a network of useful people | **Build contacts**; that's the secret. | |
| make a profit | verb phrase | /meɪk ə ˈprɒfɪt/ | tạo ra lợi nhuận | to earn more money than you spend | You may not **make a profit** immediately. Be patient. | |
| stiff competition | noun phrase | /stɪf ˌkɒmpəˈtɪʃn/ | cạnh tranh gay gắt | strong rivalry from other companies | You will probably face **stiff competition**. | |
| feedback | noun | /ˈfiːdbæk/ | phản hồi | information about what people think of a product or service | Get regular **feedback** from your employees and customers. | |
| business | noun | /ˈbɪznəs/ | việc kinh doanh / doanh nghiệp | commercial activity, or an enterprise | We hope to do more **business** in Asia next year. | |
