# 💞 Relationships — Vocabulary

| best friend | noun phrase | /ˌbest ˈfrend/ | bạn thân nhất | the one friend you feel closest to | Are you and Holly **best friends**? | |
| good friend | noun phrase | /ˌɡʊd ˈfrend/ | bạn tốt, bạn thân | a friend you like and trust | She's a **good friend** – she's not my best friend. | |
| acquaintance | noun | /əˈkweɪntəns/ | người quen | a person you have met but you don't know well | But she's more than just a casual **acquaintance**, right? | |

| steady | adjective | /ˈstedi/ | ổn định, bền vững | (of a relationship) fixed and not changing suddenly | She wasn't in a **steady** relationship with anyone at the time. | |

| despise | verb | /dɪˈspaɪz/ | khinh bỉ, coi khinh | to dislike and have no respect for someone | She **despises** anyone who lies to her. | |
| be attracted to | verb phrase | /bi əˈtræktɪd tə/ | bị thu hút bởi | to feel that you like someone romantically | He's clearly **attracted to** his new neighbour. | |

| leave someone cold | idiom | /ˌliːv ˈsʌmwʌn kəʊld/ | không gây ấn tượng, dửng dưng | to fail to interest or excite someone | Her friend just **leaves me cold** / doesn't do anything for me. | |

| fall out with | phrasal verb | /ˌfɔːl ˈaʊt wɪð/ | cãi nhau, giận nhau | to argue with someone and stop being friendly | I've **fallen out with** my parents again. | |
| have an affair | verb phrase | /ˌhæv ən əˈfeə/ | ngoại tình | to have a (usually secret) sexual relationship | Carl is **having an affair** with his boss. | |
| make it up | phrasal verb | /ˌmeɪk ɪt ˈʌp/ | làm hoà, giảng hoà | to become friends again after a quarrel | Let's try and **make it up**. | |
